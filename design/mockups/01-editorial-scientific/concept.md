# Style 01 — Editorial Scientific

> "If a Horizon-Europe deliverable were a magazine."

A sober, deeply academic look. Serif headlines (Lora), reading-paper background (#fbf9f4), masthead with issue/volume meta, drop caps and pull quotes, a 4-px double rule as the section divider used in policy journals.

## References
1. *The Economist* feature spreads — masthead + double rule + serif headlines
2. *Nature Sustainability* article layout — single-column reading width, small caps section labels
3. EU Joint Research Centre's flagship reports — institutional palette, italic decks, footnote-style metadata

## Why for SYMBA
Closest in spirit to the SYMBA Deliverable Template V3: the tool feels like a continuation of the consortium's printed deliverables, not a separate digital product. Reviewers and policy analysts (the explicit D4.1/D4.2 audience) will trust it on sight.

## Mood
Calm, slow, defensible. Optimised for **reading and reviewing**, not clicking around.

## Palette
- Ink `#0F2C4D` (deep navy)
- Paper `#fbf9f4` (warm cream background)
- Accent `#8B6F47` (warm brown — section labels, italic emphases)
- Green `#2D8B43` (CTA hover, "yes" badges, "extended" emphasis)
- Rule `#d8d2c4` (warm grey for hairlines)

## Tradeoffs
- Pro: distinctive, hard to mistake for a generic SaaS dashboard
- Con: dense — needs taste in copywriting (every word visible)
- Con: dashboard / heavy-data pages need adapted treatment (see Style 03 if that matters)
