Industrial Symbiosis,
methodically.

A monitoring and reporting system for the bio-based value chain — methodologically defensible LCSA configurations for industries, communities, local authorities, and end-users.

ENCO · CIRCE · CET — Task 4.6, WP4

The SYMBA project answers a simple but stubborn question: how do we measure, credibly, the environmental, economic, and social benefits of industries cooperating instead of competing? Each cluster of partners — a paper mill sending fly ash to a cement plant, a brewery passing spent grain to a fish farm, a port reusing a neighbour's waste heat — generates a "symbiotic dividend" that firm-by-firm accounting cannot capture.

This tool operationalises the methodological charter laid out in deliverables D4.1 (LCA Guidelines), D4.2 (LCC Guidelines), and D4.3 (S-LCA Guidelines) into a seven-question assessment. From those seven answers, an engine derives one of five terminal IS pathways (IS-01 through IS-05), activates the relevant subset of 186 methodological nodes, and produces a calibrated data-collection file ready for the network partners.

Validated case studies

Twelve papers · One scaffold
N° I.
Sokka — Kymenlaakso
Six-actor pulp & paper IES in southern Finland. ENV-only assessment, RS3 improvement scenario. Canonical IS-01 multi-actor pathway.
Sokka et al., 2011 — Finland
N° II.
Wiktor & Johansson — Malmö
VA Syd sewage sludge valorization. Most extensive multi-scenario design in the corpus: 12 alternatives + baseline, ENV+ECO dimensions.
Wiktor & Johansson, 2018 — Sweden
N° III.
Briassoulis — Bio-polymers framework
Industry-wide propositional framework for bio-based polymers. Full LCSA (ENV+ECO+SOC) plus techno-economic analysis. Q1=C policy pathway IS-02.
Briassoulis et al., 2023 — EU
N° IV.
Danielsson — Kalundborg
The iconic eight-member symbiosis, operational since 1972. Public superiority claim triggers mandatory peer review (3+ ISO-14044 experts, no weighting).
Danielsson et al., 2018 — Denmark
