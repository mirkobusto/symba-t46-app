# Style 02 — Geometric Horizon

> "If the WP-template PPTX grew up."

The literal interpretation of *"white blue and green geometric project management presentation"*: hexagons, diamonds, triangles, diagonal gradients. Bold sans (Space Grotesk) for headlines, Inter for body. Heavy use of the SYMBA blue + green + a yellow accent (#F5C518) that pulls the eye to CTAs.

## References
1. SYMBA WP_template.pptx — literal source
2. Linear / Plausible landing pages — modern marketing-saas with geometric BG SVGs
3. EU Horizon Europe webpages — gradient hero + KPI strip pattern

## Why for SYMBA
The most consortium-recognizable: a partner who has seen the WP pptx will recognize the same vocabulary on the web. Strong for **dissemination** (D4.6 is PU — needs to look "live" not "scientific").

## Mood
Confident, official, "we're a real project". Trade-off vs Style 01: less reading-friendly, more click-bait. Good for landing the casual visitor; less ideal for long methodology sessions.

## Palette
- Primary blue `#1F4E79` (matches xlsx headers)
- Green `#2D8B43`
- Accent yellow `#F5C518` (drives the eye to CTAs and the "extended" badges)
- Slate-50 `#f7f9fb` background
- Slate-200 borders

## Signature elements
- Hex SVG logo in header
- Diagonal/rotated geometric shapes in hero background (rotated squares, circles, triangles, hexagons)
- Hexagon-shaped "section number" badges
- Color-coded vertical strip on every card (blue / green)
- Yellow CTAs in the verdict banner

## Tradeoffs
- Pro: project-recognizable, energetic, dissemination-ready
- Pro: very distinct from generic SaaS — owns a visual territory
- Con: requires SVG/illustration discipline — sloppy geometric shapes look amateur
- Con: yellow accent could overwhelm if overused
